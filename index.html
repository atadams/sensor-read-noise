What am I looking at?

The bell curve shows the probability distribution of values a pixel could produce due to electronic read noise. It is centered on the pixel's true signal level (determined by how much light hit that pixel). The Canon 5D Mark II at ISO 200 has read noise of σ ≈ 5.9 ADU — that's the width of the bell curve.

The red line at 1023 ADU marks the camera's black level — the "zero light" reference. NoFakery's "Dead Zone" claim argues that noise should always produce some pixels near this line. But as you can see, in bright scenes like Jonas's cloud photo, the bell curve is hundreds of ADU away. The noise wobble (±6 ADU) is far too small to bridge a 200+ ADU gap.

Try clicking on the dark areas of the control image (the architectural interior) and notice how the bell curve sits close to or overlaps the black level line. That's why dark scenes have pixels near black level — the scene content is dark, not because the camera always generates noise there.
